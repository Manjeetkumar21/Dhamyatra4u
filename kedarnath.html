About Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India. Situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) in the Garhwal Himalayan range, it is one of the most revered shrines in the country.

The temple's legendary origins date back to the 8th century AD, when it was revived by Adi Shankaracharya, a renowned Hindu philosopher and spiritual leader. The temple's magnificent architecture, with its striking pyramidal structure and carved stone exteriors, reflects the brilliance of the ancient Indian architectural style.

Kedarnath is also considered one of the four major sites in India's revered Char Dham pilgrimage, along with Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri. Every year, thousands of devotees undertake this challenging yet spiritually rewarding journey to seek blessings from Lord Shiva.
